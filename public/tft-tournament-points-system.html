TFT Tournament Points System Explained

Understanding how points work is essential for competitive TFT. Here's the complete breakdown of the scoring system used in TFT Clash tournaments, based on the official 2026 EMEA Esports Rulebook.

Points Per Placement

In each game, every player earns points based on their final placement:

  • 1st Place: 8 points
  • 2nd Place: 7 points
  • 3rd Place: 6 points
  • 4th Place: 5 points
  • 5th Place: 4 points
  • 6th Place: 3 points
  • 7th Place: 2 points
  • 8th Place: 1 point

This means even an 8th place finish earns you something. Consistency matters more than occasional wins.

How Season Standings Work

Points accumulate across all weekly clashes throughout a season (typically 8-12 weeks). The season leaderboard shows cumulative points, with the top player crowned Season Champion.

Drop weeks: Some seasons allow your worst 1-2 weeks to be dropped from your total. This rewards regular attendance without punishing a single bad week.

Tiebreaker Rules

When two or more players have the same total points, tiebreakers are applied in this order:

  • 1. Total points — Higher points wins
  • 2. Wins + Top 4s — Wins count double (win = 2, top 4 = 1)
  • 3. Placement distribution — Most 1st places, then most 2nd places, etc.
  • 4. Most recent game — Better recent finish wins the tie

Why This System Works

The 8-1 points system with these tiebreakers rewards consistency over variance. A player who gets 4th every game (5 pts each) outscores a player who alternates between 1st (8 pts) and 8th (1 pt). This mirrors real competitive TFT where consistent top 4 finishes are the mark of a skilled player.
