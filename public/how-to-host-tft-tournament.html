How to Host a TFT Tournament

Running a Teamfight Tactics tournament does not have to be complicated. Whether it is 8 friends or 64 competitive players, this guide covers everything you need to run a clean, competitive event.

1. Choose Your Format

Swiss (Recommended for 16+ players): Everyone plays every round. Points accumulate. Best format for TFT because it rewards consistency over single-game variance.

Round-Robin: Great for smaller groups (8-16). Every player faces every lobby combination. Takes longer but gives the most accurate results.

Single Elimination: Fast and dramatic. Top 4 advance from each lobby. Works well for finals or time-constrained events.

2. Set the Rules

Use the official EMEA Esports Rulebook points system: 1st = 8pts, 2nd = 7pts, down to 8th = 1pt. This is the standard used in official Riot tournaments and what TFT Clash uses by default.

Tiebreakers (in order):

  • Total tournament points
  • Wins + top 4 finishes (wins count double)
  • Head-to-head placement comparison (1st places, then 2nd, etc.)
  • Most recent game finish

3. Managing Lobbies

For multi-lobby events, use anti-stack seeding: players who were in the same lobby last round get split into different lobbies. This prevents the same players from facing each other repeatedly.

Other seeding options: random, rank-based (strongest players distributed evenly), or snake draft.

TFT Clash handles lobby assignment automatically for all seeding modes.

4. Collecting Results

Have players enter their placements directly on the tournament page, or have an admin enter them. No screenshots needed. The platform tracks everything automatically and updates standings in real time.

5. Crowning a Champion

After all rounds are complete, finalize the standings. The platform auto-calculates points, applies tiebreakers in the correct order, and generates shareable result cards for social media.

6. Season Tracking

TFT Clash lets you run a full season: weekly clashes with cumulative standings, drop weeks, Hall of Fame for season champions, and permanent career stats for every player. No setup needed beyond creating the first clash.

Hosting Tips

  • Post a check-in deadline 30 minutes before start time. Swap no-shows to keep lobbies at 8.
  • Announce the schedule at least 48 hours in advance so players can prepare.
  • Use the broadcast channel to update players on round status in real time.
  • Pin the leaderboard link after each clash so players see updated standings immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a paid plan to host a TFT tournament?
No. Basic clash hosting for your friend group is completely free. The Host tier adds branded pages, image uploads, and unlimited custom tournament creation for larger communities.
How many players can I run in one tournament?
TFT Clash supports 8 to 64 players per clash. Lobbies are always 8 players. With 16 players you get 2 lobbies, with 32 you get 4, and so on.
Can I host a TFT tournament for my Discord server?
Yes. Create a TFT Clash account, set up your weekly clash, share the registration link with your Discord members, and manage everything from the host dashboard. Discord OAuth lets players sign in instantly.
What happens if a player disconnects mid-game?
Their placement is recorded as whatever they finished at when they left. The admin can manually adjust placements if needed before finalizing the round.
