- Community organizers use Discord + Google Sheets + Battlefy stitched together

## 2. Monetization Strategy

### Priority order (most important first)

#### #1: Subscriptions (40-50% of revenue at every scale)

This is your bread and butter. Gaming audiences will pay $5/mo for competitive tools.

**Player Pro ($4.99/mo) — what drives conversions:**
- Ad-free experience (the #1 most compelling feature for gamers)
- Advanced personal analytics (placement trends, comp tracking, improvement graphs)
- Profile customization (animated borders, custom colors, achievement showcase)
- Exclusive "Pro" badge on leaderboards and profiles
- Season recap videos/infographics (Spotify Wrapped for TFT)
- Early access to new features

**Host ($19.99/mo) — what makes it worth it:**
- Fully branded tournament pages (custom logo, colors, domain)
- Unlimited tournament creation
- Registration fee collection + payout management
- Sponsor placement slots on tournament pages
- Post-event analytics and engagement reports
- Advanced formats (Swiss, round-robin, double elimination)

**Conversion benchmarks:** Gaming platforms see 2-5% free-to-paid conversion.
- At 10K MAU with 3% = 300 subs x $4.99 = **$1,497/mo**
- Plus 15 hosts x $19.99 = **$300/mo**

#### #2: Affiliate marketing (low effort, passive)

| Category | Commission | Why it works |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| VPNs (NordVPN, Surfshark) | 40-100% of first payment | Highest paying. "Low-ping gaming VPN" recommendation |
| Gaming peripherals (Razer, Logitech, HyperX) | 3-10% | Natural fit. Gear recommendations page |
| Energy drinks (G Fuel) | 10-15% | Tournament prize giveaways |
| Gaming chairs (Secretlab) | 5-8% | High order value ($300-500) |

Start with VPN affiliates immediately — zero effort, high payout.

#### #3: Sponsorships (once you hit 5K+ MAU)

**Tier progression:**
- **$500 deal** (< 5K users): Single tournament naming, logo on results, 2-3 social posts
- **$1,000 deal**: Monthly series naming, logo across site, weekly social posts
- **$2,500 deal**: Season naming rights, prominent branding, stream integration
- **$5,000 deal**: Full season partnership, custom branded pages, co-marketed content

**Who to approach first:** Local gaming cafes, small peripheral brands, PC builders. Then endemic brands (HyperX, SteelSeries, Corsair). Energy drinks later (G Fuel is accessible; Red Bull is selective).

**What you offer sponsors:**
- Tournament naming rights (highest value)
- Logo on bracket/standings/results pages
- Custom branded tournament page (your Host product is the pitch)
- Social media mentions
- Newsletter mentions

#### #4: Entry fee commission (after legal setup)

The FACEIT model for TFT:
- 5-10% platform commission (8% is standard)
- $5 entry x 8 players = $40 pool. 8% = $3.20 to platform
- At scale: 100 tournaments/week = ~$1,400/mo
- **Important:** Don't rush this. Legal complexity is real (see Legal section below)

#### #5: Ads (last priority, lowest value)

Gaming CPMs are among the lowest ($0.50-$3.00) and **60%+ of gamers use ad blockers**.

**If you add ads:**
1. Start with **Google AdSense** (no minimum traffic, $1-4 RPM)
2. Move to **NitroPay** at 15K+ daily pageviews ($3-8 RPM, Net 7 payments)
3. Upgrade to **Venatus or Playwire** at 100K+ monthly pageviews ($5-15 RPM)

**Where to place ads (dark UI best practices):**
- Results pages (users lingering, reading results — high ad tolerance)
- Between tournament rounds (natural content break)
- Archive/history pages (browsing mode)
- Player profile pages (discovery mode)
- **NEVER** on live bracket during active play

**How to not ruin your dark theme:**
- Dark-themed ad containers matching `#111827` panel color
- Subtle `#9B72CF` accent borders so ads feel native
- Label subtly with small "AD" tag in your purple
- Offer Pro subscription as the ad-free alternative — converts ad-blocker users into paying customers

## 3. Growth Strategy

### SEO — The #1 long-term growth channel

**Critical problem:** Your React SPA is invisible to Google. Google sees an empty `<div id="root">`. Fix options:

1. **Prerender.io** (quickest, ~$9/mo) — serves static HTML to crawlers
2. **react-snap** — pre-generates static HTML at build time
3. **Next.js migration** (best long-term) — full SSR/SSG

**Keywords to own (low competition, high intent):**
- "TFT tournament" / "TFT online tournament" / "weekly TFT tournament"
- "how to host a TFT tournament" / "TFT tournament format"
- "TFT clash rules" / "TFT competitive rules" / "TFT points system"
- "TFT community tournament" / "free TFT tournament platform"

**Programmatic SEO (the lolchess playbook):**
Every tournament result, player profile, and season standing should be a unique indexable URL:
- `/results/season-1/week-12` — full results, standings
- `/player/levitate` — career stats, achievements
- `/season/1/standings` — cumulative leaderboard
- Each page = another long-tail SEO asset that compounds over time

**Google Discover opportunity:** Gaming content saw 138% traffic growth on Discover through 2025. Auto-generated weekly recaps with hero images (1200x628px+) can trigger Discover placement.

### Community growth playbook (0 to 10K)

**Phase 1 — Core 20 (Weeks 1-4):** You already have this (your friend group). Each player invites 2-3 from their TFT circles. Run 3-4 clashes to prove the format.

**Phase 2 — Discord engine (Weeks 4-12):**
- Build/integrate a Discord bot for registration, check-in, results posting
- Channels: `#clash-registration`, `#results`, `#general`, `#looking-for-group`, `#clips`
- Tiered roles based on participation (Rookie, Regular, Veteran, Champion)
- Automated notifications: registration open, 1hr reminder, results posted

**Phase 3 — Reddit infiltration (Weeks 4-16):**
- r/CompetitiveTFT (200K+ members) is the highest-value organic channel
- **Do NOT spam-promote.** Post genuinely useful content:
  - "We ran a 16-player Swiss format — here's what we learned about tiebreakers"
  - "After 12 weeks of community clashes, here are the most consistent placements by rank"
  - Tournament recaps with interesting narratives
- Build credibility first, then occasional posts like "Free weekly TFT clashes — open to anyone"

**Phase 4 — Twitter/X and short-form (Weeks 8-20):**
- TFT competitive community is active on Twitter. Share results, tag participants
- TikTok (2.34% engagement rate) > YouTube Shorts (0.91%) for virality
- 15-30 second clips: dramatic finishes, close standings, highlight moments

**Phase 5 — Micro-streamers (Weeks 12-24):**
- Target streamers with 500-5K Twitch viewers (accessible, looking for community events)
- Offer to run "streamer invitational" clashes — they stream, you handle logistics
- Creator codes/referral links to track signups they drive

**Phase 6 — Cross-pollination (Ongoing):**
- Partner with existing TFT Discord communities to host clashes for their members
- University/college esports clubs (TFT = perfect campus format: individual, low barrier)

1. **Auto-generated shareable images** (highest impact):
   - Match recap cards (1080x1080): placement, stats, comp played
   - Season stats cards: "Levitate — Season 1 | 16 Wins | 1024pts | Challenger"

2. **One-click sharing:**
   - Twitter/Discord share buttons with pre-formatted text

   - OG meta tags on every shareable page

3. **Referral program:**
   - "Bring a friend" — both get bonus badge when referred player completes first clash
   - Leaderboard of top referrers (competitive gamers will compete at referring too)
   - Squad bonus: register as group of 4+, everyone gets "Squad" badge

### Retention — the appointment gaming model

TFT Clash's weekly cadence is its **greatest structural advantage**:
- Fixed weekly schedule = players build it into routine
- Season structure (8-12 weeks) = narrative arcs and stakes
- Cumulative points = natural FOMO without being punitive
- Season finale = higher stakes championship clash
- Missing a week means falling behind, but drop-weeks make it forgiving

**Notification cadence (3/week max — 46% of users disable at 2-5/week):**
1. Registration opens (48h before)
2. Reminder (2h before)
3. Results posted (after clash)

## 4. Riot Games Ecosystem

### Developer API

- Register at developer.riotgames.com for TFT API key

- Apply for Production API key once you have a working product
- Available data: match history, player stats, leaderboards, spectator data

**Integration roadmap:**

4. Historical stats integration

### Tournament licensing

- **Tier 3 (Community License):** Non-commercial, strict limits, no sponsors, easiest approval
- **Tier 2 (Custom License):** Commercial, moderate limits, must not conflict with Riot events
- Must include: "Not endorsed by Riot Games"
- Community leaderboards and rankings are **explicitly allowed**
- Use their Small Tournament Asset Pack for promotion

### Creator Partner Program

- Currently in closed beta (launched September 2025)
- Applications open globally late 2026
- Build your portfolio of organized tournaments now so you have a track record when applications open

### EMEA Community Framework (new for 2026)

Riot launched a three-tier community structure:
1. **Community Events** — For content creators, tournament organizers. Must validate 14+ days before
2. **TFT Minors** — France, Spain, Germany, Poland only currently
3. **TFT Majors** — Larger events with official Riot support

**Opportunity:** Be the platform Riot points community organizers to. They want organizers to professionalize but there are no tools. TFT Clash IS the tool.

## 5. Legal Considerations

### Entry fee tournaments

**TFT is skill-predominant** — strategy, economy, positioning. This means entry fee competitions are generally NOT gambling.

- **US:** Legal in ~40 states. Restricted in AZ, AR, CT, DE, LA, MT, SC, SD, TN
- **EU:** No single law — each member state decides. Generally exempt from gambling regulation
- **UK:** Generally legal under Gambling Act 2005 for skill-based competitions
- **Germany:** Strictest in EU (GlüStV 2021), may need licensing

**Safest approach:**
1. Start with free-entry tournaments only
2. When adding paid entry, use "platform fee" model (not entry-fee-to-prize-pool)
3. Geo-restrict paid tournaments to verified jurisdictions
4. Consult a lawyer before launching paid entry anywhere

### GDPR (EU users)

- Implement: Right to Access, Right to Deletion, Right to Data Portability
- Use Supabase EU region for data residency
- Write clear privacy policy
- Add cookie consent banner
- Build delete-account functionality

### When to incorporate

**Before you process any money** — subscriptions, entry fees, or sponsorships.
- EU: Ltd (UK), BV (Netherlands), GmbH (Germany), SAS (France)
- US: Delaware LLC or C-Corp if seeking VC
- Cost: $100-500 for formation, $50-200/yr registered agent
